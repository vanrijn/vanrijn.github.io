[![arcade_stick]({{site.baseurl}}/assets/2005/03/3068418508_086f77f1f7_o.jpg)]({{site.baseurl}}/assets/2005/03/3068418508_086f77f1f7_o.jpg "arcade_stick by vanRijn") So, I've done a bunch of research and [question-asking](http://www.shoryuken.com/forums/showthread.php?p=2227363&posted=1) and reading and cursing and [gamestopping](http://www.gamestop.com) and some more reading and some more [question-asking](http://forums.xbox.com/ShowPost.aspx?PostID=11954052), etc. When I originally built my custom arcade sticks, I used the PCB's from old PSX digital-only controllers, since they were the easiest to hack and solder. These worked really well for me (in combination with some PSX-to-USB converters I got from Radio Shack for $10) with PC games. Both Linux and Windows see the arcade sticks as regular joystick devices and they work perfectly fine with [M.A.M.E.](http://www.mame.net) and [the TOTALLY cool Killer Instinct emulator](http://www.sys2064.com/u64.htm).

Anyway, as I've blogged much about before, along came the Xbox and Street Fighter Anniversary Collection into my life and with it my hopes that I could use my arcade sticks with it. And, as I've blogged much about before, this dream was smashed to oblivion due to the [Dreambox converters](http://www.lik-sang.com/info.php?products_id=1860&=23516) that I bought from LikSang being unable to work with non-analog PSX controllers.

So I modded a new controller a few nights ago (Gamestop is wonderful!) and replaced the digital PCB for my arcade stick with it. I used [SpiffyShoes PSX A-series controller hack](http://web.archive.org/web/20070310042602/http://home.comcast.net:80/~spiffyshoes/DualShockHack/) which lets you do a completely no-solder hack on a A-series dual-shock PS1 controller. I only had some spare CAT-5 lying around to use for the wire, and it was REALLY painfully slow and frustrating trying to use its 24-gauge twisted-pair wires to plug into the ribbon terminal. I'm not completely happy with it, as it looks a bit unstable, not having the wires actually secured to the terminal in any way, and I'm not sure if I want to leave it as it is or replace it with a soldered PS1 H-series PCB, but at the very least, I hooked it up to SFAC through my Dreambox converter and it works perfectly.

[Here are some pictures of my mod](http://flickr.com/photos/vr/sets/439496/) (not pretty on the inside, I admit it), including the [closeup of the no-solder PCB board](http://flickr.com/photos/vr/18621301/in/set-439496/). I'm worried that the wires are going to come out one of these days soon.

But for now, I'm happy being able to use the little bugger on Xbox live. =:)

Happy gaming....
